Identifying Usability Problems:

This is one of my family's favorite places to get takeout back at home. I thought their website could use a makeover, so I went and did it!

Perceived Usability Issues:
  • Learnability: The unconventional orientation of the website makes it hard to learn.
  • Usability: The ordering interface is embedded into the website, making it pretty small
  • Usability: The ordering interface has no back buttons
  • Usability: The ordering interface doesn't show you what is currently in your cart.
  • Usability: The ordering interface has uninuitive linkages between buttons
  • Usability: The ordering interface generally requires a lot of jumping around
  • Usability: The alert is quite hard to read given the contrast between the colors.
  • Memorability: The site is generally hard to re-orient to when coming back after a while, due to its disorganization
  • Memorability: There are different brandings between the website and the in-person menu
WAVE Issues:
  • Errors: Missing alternative texts, missing html language, 6 contrast errors
  • Alerts: No page regions, suspicious links
  • Arias: None

Overall, I agree with the majority of what WAVE is showing. It points out a lot of accessibility issues concerning screen readers and low contrast, for those who are visually impaired, and these characteristics were things that I had previously overlooked.
